Intuition

  • In linear regression, we find the line that minimizes the sum of squared errors.
  • This can be viewed from a probabilistic perspective: where the errors are normally distributed, and we're finding the parameters that maximize the likelihood of observing the data. The probability of observing our data is max'd when the predicted values from the regression line are as close as possible to the observed values, given the assumed distribution of errors.
