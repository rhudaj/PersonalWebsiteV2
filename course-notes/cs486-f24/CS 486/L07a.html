Uncertainty

The world has uncertainty (e.g. noise in sensor). But, agents can still make decisions by knowing how uncertain they are (i.e. knowing how bad your estimate is helps you).

  • probability is a measure of belief
    • P(x)=0 or 1P(x)=0 \text{ or } 1P(x)=0 or 1﻿ ⇒ you are certain (no belief involved)
    • 0<P(x)<10<P(x)<10<P(x)<1﻿ ⇒ you have a belief
  • You can also see it as a measure of ignorance
    • P(x)≈0.5P(x) \approx 0.5P(x)≈0.5﻿ ⇒ you don’t know much
    • P(x)≈0 or 1P(x) \approx 0 \text{ or }1 P(x)≈0 or 1﻿ ⇒ you’re fairly confident

    Should you wear a seatbelt?

    • Frequentist: drives once, no crash. Drives again, no crash. So doesn't wear a seatbelt until they get into a crash.
    • Bayesian: has prior believe that there is a high chance of crashing (e.g. based on news reports), so starts with a seatbelt. Every time they drive and don’t crash, they are more keen on not wearing a seatbelt.
