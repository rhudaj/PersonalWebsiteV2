Problem

Properties

Dijkstra’s Algorithm

Idea

Until you’ve visited every vertex:

  • Look at the unseen vertex w’ the min distance (sss﻿ at first)
  • For every other vertex it has an edge to, update their parent/weight if shorter.
  • More Detail

    Vertices store:

    • d[v]d[v]d[v]﻿ : current shortest path from sss﻿
    • π[v]\pi[v]π[v]﻿ : predecessor in the path (or NILNILNIL﻿)

    Edges store:

    • w(e)w(e)w(e)﻿ : weight

Analogy: fire at each vertex, vvv﻿, water at sss﻿, edge weight = time it takes for water to flow from one end to the other. Shortest path = quickest way for water to put out the fire.

  • algorithm finds which fire will be put out next.

Algorithm

Complexity

  • Each vertex is enqueued once (in the beginning) & dequeued once (finding the min)

    ⇒ n⋅cost(enqueue/dequeue)n \cdot \text{cost(enqueue/dequeue)}n⋅cost(enqueue/dequeue)﻿

  • Evertime we dequeue
    • we check every edge from it (once)
    • we may need to update the key value in the heap

    ⇒ m⋅cost(update key)m \cdot \text{cost(update key)}m⋅cost(update key)﻿

Thus, (n+m)⋅cost(enqueue/dequeue)(n+m) \cdot \text{cost(enqueue/dequeue)}(n+m)⋅cost(enqueue/dequeue)﻿
