Idea: given a signal (pressure vs. time), decompose it into frequencies (sinusoids that when added, produce the original signal).

How: create a math-machine that treats signals with a different frequency differently from how it treats other signals.

Formula for “Center of Mass”

Take the integral, divide by the total time → gives you the average center of mass for every point in time → center of mass

But the actual Fourier Transform does not divide by the total duration.

  • what does this mean?
    • the magnitude of the Fourier Transform is scaled up more and more for frequencies that persist for longer.
    • So if you have a clear frequency but it’s only for 1 second, over time, it will be negligeable.

FFT Output

The position of each item in the FFT output (array) corresponds to some frequency:

  • kkk﻿ — index
  • NNN﻿ — total number of items in the list
  • FsFsFs﻿ — Sampling Rate

Each item is complex number:

  • real — cosine component
  • imaginary — sine component
  • The value represents the amplitude of this wave
  • The sign tells us about the phase of this wave

When combined, we can figure out what sinusoid it represents:

Frequency

The frequency is:

F=kN×FsF=\frac{k}{N} \times F_sF=Nk​×Fs​﻿

Magnitude

Not amplitude. Amplitude is +/-, magnitude is only positive (tells us about scale).

magnitude=real2+imag2\text{magnitude}= \sqrt{\text{real}^2+\text{imag}^2}magnitude=real2+imag2​﻿

Phase

The sign of the amplitude tells us about phase:

phase=tan⁡−1(imag/real)\text{phase}=\tan^-1(\text{imag}/\text{real})phase=tan−1(imag/real)﻿
