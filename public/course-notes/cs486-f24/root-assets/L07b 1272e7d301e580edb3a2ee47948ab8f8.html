Var. Elim. Algorithm

  • Variable Ordering — Polytree's

    The algorithm is exponential in terms of the size of the largest factor. We can reduce size of factors by picking a good ordering.

    • For a Polytree (at most 1 path between any two nodes). It's optimal to work outside in; start eliminating from the outermost variables and work toward the center. This minimizes factor growth based on the simple structure.
    • Eliminate singly-connected nodes (D,A,C,X1,…,Xk)(D, A, C, X_1, \dots , X_k)(D,A,C,X1​,…,Xk​)﻿ first ⇒ no factor is ever larger than original CPTs.
    • If you eliminate B first, a large factor is created that includes: A,C,X1,...,XkA, C, X_1, . . . , X_kA,C,X1​,...,Xk​﻿.

  • Relevant Variables

    For a given query, some variables may not influence the result, so we can safely ignore them, reducing the complexity of our calculation.

    • Example
      • To compute P(A)P(A)P(A)﻿, we don’t need to sum over BBB﻿ and CCC﻿

    Given, Q,EQ,EQ,E﻿, the relevant variables are:

    1. QQQ﻿
    1. Any parent of a relevant variable.
    1. EEE﻿, if any descendent is relevant

    * While this approach can help by eliminating irrelevant variables, it doesn’t always simplify the network completely. Some variables that appear necessary for the computation may still have no effect, but the rules above don’t account for all such cases.
