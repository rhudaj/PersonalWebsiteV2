Autocorrelation (pitch contours)

Sums the multiplication of the signal by the shifted signal.

The greater the value ⇒ the closer the 2 signals are.

ACF:

Lag Estimation

Maximizing the ACF ⇒ RmaxR_{max}Rmax​﻿

  • the highest peak indicates the time shift to get the highest autocorelation value
  • in other words, this is the pitch period
  • note: the first peak at T = 0 should not be counted

Unvoiced vs. Voiced Signal

Voiced → has periodic patterns → has a f0f_0f0​﻿ and a pitch

  • the ACF would have a high peak at T=0T=0T=0﻿ and fizzle out quickly

Unvoiced → no periodic patterns → just noise → has no f0f_0f0​﻿ or pitch

  • the ACF would have a high peak at T=0T=0T=0﻿, and peak again

Rule

Rmax<αR(0)  ⟹  unvoicedR_{max} < \alpha R(0) \implies \text{unvoiced}Rmax​<αR(0)⟹unvoiced﻿

Rmax≥αR(0)  ⟹  voicedR_{max} \ge \alpha R(0) \implies \text{voiced}Rmax​≥αR(0)⟹voiced﻿
